# **Safety Information**

The following safety instructions, general precautions, and rules concerning handling and the operating environment must be strictly observed to ensure personnel safety, product integrity, and correct system operation.

```{warning}
**User responsibility**

Compliance with all safety rules listed in this section is mandatory and is the responsibility of the end user. Failure to comply may cause injury to personnel, damage to equipment, or impair system operation.
```

---

## Operational safety

### Integration with robotic systems

#### **Cell safety requirements**

```{warning}
FlexiVision One operates in close connection with third-party robotic systems. The user must ensure that the work area is equipped with all necessary safety measures required by the applicable regulations
```
#### **Attention during operation**

```{warning}

During system operation, always take into account:

- Physical footprint of the robot and FlexiBowl
- Trajectories and speeds of robotic movements
- Possible unforeseen situations (falling parts, picking errors)
- Hazard zones during FlexiBowl vibration phases
```

### General precautions before interventions

#### **Disconnecting power supplies**

```{warning}
Before performing any maintenance, modification, or inspection work on the system, always make sure that:

- All electrical power sources are disconnected (VisionController, FlexiBowl, Camera, Illuminator)
- The pneumatic supply is discharged and disconnected (if present)
- Connection cables are physically disconnected
- The robot is in safety mode or completely switched off
```
#### **Safety procedures**

```{warning}

Do not rely exclusively on switches: use lockout/tagout (LOTO) procedures when available.
```

### Modifications and tampering

#### **Prohibition of unauthorized modifications**

```{warning}
Never modify the product or its components without express written authorization from ARS S.r.l.
```
#### **Consequences of modifications**

```{warning}
Unauthorized modifications may:

- Cause system malfunctions
- Void the warranty
- Create risks of injury, electric shock, or fire
- Compromise the product's safety certifications
```

## Environmental conditions and protection

### Protection against liquids

#### **Risk of contact with liquids**

```{warning}

Do not use the product in environments where the VisionController, camera, or other electronic components may come into contact with:

- Water drops or splashes
- Oils, lubricants, or other industrial liquids
- Condensation or excessive humidity
- Conductive dust
```
#### **Solutions for critical environments**

```{note}

If the system must operate in environments where liquids are present, provide suitable protection (IP65 or higher enclosures) and consult the ARS technical service for customized solutions.
```

### Operating temperatures

#### **Hot surfaces - Maximum temperatures**

```{warning}
Under intensive use conditions or in hot environments, some system components may reach high temperatures:

- VisionController: up to 50°C on external surfaces
- LED illuminator: up to 40°C on the front surface
- Industrial camera: up to 50°C on the metal body
```
#### **Customer responsibility**

```{warning}
It is the customer's responsibility to:

- Document thermal risks in their own risk assessment
- Instruct personnel on procedures to avoid accidental contact
- Provide warning signs where necessary
- Ensure adequate ventilation of the components
```

### Environmental conditions for installation and storage

#### **Environmental requirements - Reference table**

```{note}

To ensure durability and reliability, the VisionController and camera must be used and stored under the following conditions:

| Parameter | Operating conditions | Storage conditions |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| **Temperature** | +1°C ÷ +50°C | -20°C ÷ +65°C |
| **Relative humidity** | <90% (without condensation) | <90% (without condensation) |


```
#### **Additional environmental precautions**

```{note}
To preserve component integrity:

- Avoid direct exposure to sunlight
- Protect against excessive vibration during storage
- Keep in a dry environment free from aggressive dust
- The camera is sensitive to mechanical shock: handle with care
```

## Transport and handling

### Receipt and inspection

#### **Inspection upon arrival**

```{note}
Upon receipt of the product, before signing the delivery note:

1. **External inspection of the packaging**: Check the integrity of the box and external packaging. Check for any signs of impact, crushing, or wetting.

2. **Content check**: Compare the contents with the delivery note. Check that all ordered components are present.
```

#### **In case of damage or discrepancies**

```{note}
If any problems are found:

- DO NOT sign the receipt as "conforming"
- Record the damage on the transport document
- Photograph any visible damage
- Contact ARS support immediately: 
    [service@arsautomation.com](mailto:service@arsautomation.com) 
    [us.service@arsautomation.com](mailto:us.service@arsautomation.com) if contacting from America
```

### Handling and storage
To prevent damage during transport and storage:

#### **Transport**

```{tip}
**During transport:**
- Always handle the packaging in an upright position (respect the "UP" arrows on the packaging)
- Do not drop or impact the package
- Use carts or pallet trucks suitable for the weight
- Avoid sudden temperature changes
```
#### **Storage**

```{tip}
**During storage:**
- Store in a dry and covered place
- Do not stack other loads on the packaging
- Do not climb on or lean against the packaging
- Respect the environmental conditions indicated in the previous table
```
#### **Unpacking**

```{tip}
**During unpacking:**
- Open carefully to avoid damaging the internal components
- Keep the original packaging for any returns or future transport
- Check that all accessories and documentation are present
```

## Disposal and end of life

### **Responsible disposal**

```{warning}

When the product reaches the end of its life cycle, it must be disposed of in accordance with the current regulations concerning waste electrical and electronic equipment (RAEE/WEEE).
```
### **Components subject to special disposal**

```{note}
**Components subject to special disposal:**
- Electronic boards (VisionController): RAEE category 6
- Industrial camera: RAEE category 6
- LED illuminators: RAEE category 5
- Cables and connectors: disposal with electrical materials
```
