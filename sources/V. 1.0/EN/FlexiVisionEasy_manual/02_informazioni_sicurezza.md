# **Safety Information**

The following safety instructions, general precautions, and rules regarding handling and the operating environment must be strictly followed to ensure personnel safety, product integrity, and proper system operation.

```{warning}
**User responsibility**

Compliance with all safety regulations described in this section is mandatory and is the responsibility of the end user. Failure to comply may cause injury, equipment damage, or system malfunction.
```

---

## Operational safety

### Integration with robotic systems

#### **Cell safety requirements**

```{warning}
FlexiVision One operates in close connection with third-party robotic systems. The user must ensure that the work area is equipped with all safety measures required by the applicable regulations.
```

#### **Attention during operation**

```{warning}

During system operation, always consider:

- The physical footprint of the robot and the FlexiBowl
- The trajectories and speed of robotic movements
- Possible unexpected situations (falling parts, picking errors)
- Hazard zones during FlexiBowl vibration phases
```

### General precautions before intervention

#### **Disconnecting power sources**

```{warning}
Before carrying out any maintenance, modification, or inspection on the system, always make sure that:

- All electrical power sources are disconnected (VisionController, FlexiBowl, Camera, Illuminator)
- The pneumatic supply is discharged and disconnected, if present
- Connection cables are physically unplugged
- The robot is in safety mode or completely powered off
```

#### **Safety procedures**

```{warning}

Do not rely exclusively on switches: use lockout/tagout (LOTO) procedures whenever available.
```

### Modifications and tampering

#### **No unauthorized modifications**

```{warning}
Never modify the product or its components without explicit written authorization from ARS S.r.l.
```

#### **Consequences of modifications**

```{warning}
Unauthorized modifications may:

- Cause system malfunctions
- Void the warranty
- Create risks of injury, electric shock, or fire
- Compromise product safety certifications
```

## Environmental conditions and protection

### Protection against liquids

#### **Risk of contact with liquids**

```{warning}

Do not use the product in environments where the VisionController, camera, or other electronic components may come into contact with:

- Water drops or splashes
- Oils, lubricants, or other industrial liquids
- Condensation or excessive humidity
- Conductive dust
```

#### **Solutions for critical environments**

```{note}

If the system must operate in environments where liquids are present, provide appropriate protection (IP65 enclosures or higher) and consult ARS technical support for customized solutions.
```

### Operating temperatures

#### **Hot surfaces - Maximum temperatures**

```{warning}
Under heavy use or in hot environments, some system components may reach high temperatures:

- VisionController: up to 50°C on external surfaces
- LED illuminator: up to 40°C on the front surface
- Industrial camera: up to 50°C on the metal body
```

#### **Customer responsibility**

```{warning}
It is the customer's responsibility to:

- Document thermal risks in their own risk assessment
- Train personnel on procedures to avoid accidental contact
- Provide warning signs where necessary
- Ensure adequate ventilation for the components
```

### Environmental conditions for installation and storage

#### **Environmental requirements - Reference table**

```{note}

To ensure durability and reliability, the VisionController and camera must be used and stored under the following conditions:

| Parameter | Operating conditions | Storage conditions |
|-----------|----------------------|--------------------|
| **Temperature** | +1°C to +50°C | -20°C to +65°C |
| **Relative humidity** | <90% (non-condensing) | <90% (non-condensing) |


```

#### **Additional environmental precautions**

```{note}
To preserve component integrity:

- Avoid direct exposure to sunlight
- Protect against excessive vibration during storage
- Keep in a dry environment free from aggressive dust
- The camera is sensitive to mechanical shock: handle with care
```

## Transport and handling

### Receiving and inspection

#### **Inspection upon arrival**

```{note}
When the product is delivered, before signing the delivery note:

1. **External inspection of the packaging**: Check the integrity of the box and external packaging. Look for any signs of impacts, crushing, or moisture.

2. **Content verification**: Compare the content with the delivery note. Make sure that all ordered components are present.
```

#### **In case of damage or discrepancies**

```{note}
If any problems are found:

- DO NOT sign the receipt as "compliant"
- Record the damage on the transport document
- Photograph any visible damage
- Immediately contact ARS service support:
    [service@arsautomation.com](mailto:service@arsautomation.com)
    [us.service@arsautomation.com](mailto:us.service@arsautomation.com) if you are contacting us from the Americas
```

### Handling and storage
To prevent damage during transport and storage:

#### **Transport**

```{tip}
**During transport:**
- Always handle the package in an upright position (follow the "UP" arrows on the packaging)
- Do not drop or strike the package
- Use carts or pallet trucks suitable for the weight
- Avoid sudden temperature changes
```

#### **Storage**

```{tip}
**During storage:**
- Store in a dry, covered place
- Do not stack other loads on top of the package
- Do not stand or lean on the package
- Respect the environmental conditions indicated in the table above
```

#### **Unpacking**

```{tip}
**During unpacking:**
- Open carefully to avoid damaging internal components
- Keep the original packaging for possible returns or future transport
- Verify that all accessories and documentation are present
```

## Disposal and end of life

### **Responsible disposal**

```{warning}

When the product reaches the end of its life cycle, it must be disposed of in compliance with the applicable regulations for waste electrical and electronic equipment (WEEE).
```

### **Components subject to special disposal**

```{note}
**Components subject to special disposal:**
- Electronic boards (VisionController): WEEE category 6
- Industrial camera: WEEE category 6
- LED illuminators: WEEE category 5
- Cables and connectors: dispose of with electrical materials
```
